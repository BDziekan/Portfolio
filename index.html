Project Title: Austin Animal Centre Dashboard

Project Overview: An Excel dashboard analyzing animal shelter data from the Austin Animal Center, designed to uncover trends in intakes, outcomes, and shelter duration to support operational improvements.

Approach: Imported and cleaned intake and outcome CSV datasets, resolving text-formatted dates and missing values. Merged data using XLOOKUP and INDEX MATCH to link records, calculated days in shelter, and created PivotTables to summarize trends. Built an interactive dashboard with line, bar, pie, and column charts, enhanced with slicers for filtering by animal type and intake type.

Key Insights:

  • Intakes peaked in summer months, with dogs and cats dominating (e.g., Domestic Shorthair, Labrador Retriever).
  • Adoptions were the most common outcome (40% for dogs), while transfers averaged the shortest shelter time (10 days).
  • Bats appeared frequently among "Other" animals due to rabies evaluations.
  • [Placeholder: Additional insight about shelter trends]

Business Impact: Provided actionable insights for shelter management, enabling targeted adoption campaigns and resource allocation, potentially reducing shelter overcrowding and improving adoption rates.
